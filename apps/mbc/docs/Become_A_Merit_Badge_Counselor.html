Become a Merit Badge Counselor

Troops 12 & 32 Acton

Are you passionate about sharing your knowledge with youth? Becoming a Merit Badge Counselor in Scouting America is a great way to mentor and guide Scouts through their learning journey.

Eligibility

  • Be 18 or older and of good character
  • Be proficient in the subject matter
  • Be registered with Scouting America as a Merit Badge Counselor (Postion Code 42)
  • Complete and maintain Safeguarding Youth Training (SYT), formerly Youth Protection Training (YPT)

Steps to Become a Merit Badge Counselor

  1. Complete Safeguarding Youth Training at my.scouting.org. Save your certificate.
  2. Fill out the Adult Application (PDF). Specify Position Code 42 and Position Title "Merit Badge Counselor" on page 3, titled "SCOUTING AMERICA ADULT APPLICATION". If you are already registered with Scouting America, you only need to complete this page; otherwise, you must complete the entire application, including background check authorization.
  3. Review the list of merit badges and pamphlets at scouting.org/skills/merit-badges/all/.
  4. Complete the Merit Badge Counselor Information Form (PDF), listing the merit badges you want to instruct and your qualifications.
  5. Complete the following training for Merit Badge Counselors (68 minutes total) at my.scouting.org:
    • Merit Badge Counselor Position Specific Training
    • Merit Badge Counselors - Before First Meeting
    • Merit Badge Counselors - Position Trained
  6. Submit all documents to the advancement team, listed below, for review. From there, we'll send them to Lori Sullivan in the Heart of New England (HNE) Council office who will update Scoutbook.
